Most people overcomplicate health. They jump to supplements and biohacks before nailing the fundamentals.

## The basics matter most

Before worrying about anything advanced, get these right:

1. **Sleep 7-9 hours** in a cool, dark room
2. **Get morning sunlight** within 30 minutes of waking
3. **Move your body** for at least 30 minutes daily
4. **Eat whole foods** — mostly plants, quality protein, healthy fats
5. **Hydrate properly** — half your bodyweight in ounces of water

## The 80/20 of supplements

If you have the basics dialed, these are worth considering:

- **Magnesium** — most people are deficient
- **Vitamin D3 + K2** — especially if you're not getting enough sun
- **Omega-3s** — from fish oil or quality seafood

## The meta-lesson

Health optimization isn't about finding the one weird trick. It's about consistently doing boring, fundamental things well. The people who look for shortcuts usually end up further behind than those who just do the basics every day.
