After evaluating hundreds of startups, I've noticed consistent patterns in the founders who succeed.

## Speed of execution

The best founders ship fast. Not recklessly — but they have an intense bias toward action. They'd rather launch something imperfect and learn from real users than spend months perfecting something in isolation.

## Deep customer empathy

Great founders can articulate their customer's problems better than the customer can. They've spent real time talking to users, understanding their workflows, and identifying the pain points that matter most.

## Intellectual honesty

The best founders are brutally honest about what's working and what isn't. They don't fall in love with their ideas — they fall in love with solving the problem. When the data says their approach isn't working, they pivot without ego.

## Resilience without delusion

Startups are hard. The best founders keep going through the hard parts, but they're not blindly optimistic. They acknowledge reality while maintaining the belief that they can change it.

## What doesn't matter as much

- Pedigree (school, prior companies)
- Technical brilliance in isolation
- Having the "perfect" idea from day one

The founder matters more than the idea. A great founder with a mediocre idea will iterate their way to something great. A mediocre founder with a great idea will usually find a way to mess it up.
